About Me

A passionate researcher in Software Engineering focusing on code quality and software reuse

Software Engineering Salerno Lab

Active member of the SeSa Lab. Here I develop innovative research in software engineering.

Giammaria Giordano is a researcher at the SeSa Lab, University of Salerno. His work lies at the intersection of Empirical Software Engineering and Software Engineering for AI, with a focus on code quality, technical debt, and software maintainability. He has conducted large-scale empirical studies on the evolution of code smells, design patterns, and reusability mechanisms, as well as on Python- and ML-specific quality issues. His recent research investigates fairness-aware practices, testing strategies for deep learning, and the lifecycle of ML-specific code smells. He also explores security and privacy challenges in mobile and IoT systems, leveraging AI and search-based techniques to support vulnerability detection and security testing.
